Fig. 2

Surface air temperature anomalies in the zonally redistributed heat flux simulations. The NP-Cool/NA-Warm simulation ( a) prescribes a negative heat flux perturbation in North Pacific (−1 W/m 2 global) compensated with an equivalent positive North Atlantic Q-flux perturbation (+1 W/m 2 global), such that the net global energy perturbation is zero. The sign of the heat flux perturbations in the NP-Warm/NA-Cool simulation ( b) are reversed relative to the NP-Cool/NA-Warm simulation. In both simulations, the North Pacific heat flux anomaly dominates the surface air temperature response in the Artic. Gray areas indicate changes not significant at the 95% confidence level

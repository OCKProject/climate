Aim and objectives

To search the literature to identify educational approaches that have been applied to raise awareness and increase knowledge about climate change and health between 2005 and 2017. This will enable understanding of the key aspects of climate change and health education, to map strengths, weaknesses, gaps and observations in their implementation approach.

Objectives of this review are to:

Provide a comprehensive overview of any study on any educational approach used to enhance knowledge of the public on the direct and/or indirect health effects of climate change.

Disseminate review findings on the published literature about education on the health effects of climate change.

Develop recommendations for future climate change and health teaching, research and policy.

Develop propositions facilitating collaboration between learners, educators and various other stakeholders including academia and industry.

Provide a composite overview of evaluation of existing teaching methods and the associated strengths and weaknesses applied to climate change and health education.

Identify how the needs of the public and educators may be aligned, thereby enhancing knowledge on climate change and health.

Identify how teaching methods could be applied within a digital context to strengthen engagement and sustainable action by learners.

Assist the European Institute of Innovation & Technology (EIT) Health and its education partners in the development of a FutureLearn MOOC on climate change and health by applying and disseminating review findings.

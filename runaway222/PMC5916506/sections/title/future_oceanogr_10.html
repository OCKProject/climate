Future oceanographic modeling

The potential future changes in ocean surface storm waves were quantitatively simulated using models driven by historical hindcast data and projected conditions based on GCM output ( 19). Three-hour GCM wind speed and wind direction output were assimilated for the area encompassing Kwajalein Atoll from the Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 5 [CMIP5; ( 35)]. To reduce uncertainty in model variability, wind speed and wind direction for the RCP4.5 and RCP8.5 climate scenarios (table S1) from four different GCMs (table S3) were used as boundary conditions to the physics-based WAVEWATCH III ( 36) numerical wave model. Modeled time periods were limited to the years 2026–2045 and 2085–2100, as prescribed by the CMIP5 modeling framework, and interpolated to the 2035–2105 time frame at 10-year time steps. The resulting modeled deep-water wave heights, wave periods, wave directions, wind speeds, and wind directions were analyzed using published methods ( 19).

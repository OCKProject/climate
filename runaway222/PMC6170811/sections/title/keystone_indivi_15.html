Keystone individuals

The presence of influential individuals impacts social tipping points. For instance, the presence of leaders or reconciliatory individuals may prevent tit-for-tat feedback loops from ever starting [ 41, 42]. In contrast, the presence of particularly aggressive, hungry, or bold individuals could increase within-group conflict [ 43], thus changing the environmental parameter values that result in a tipping point and the feedback strength that underlies them.

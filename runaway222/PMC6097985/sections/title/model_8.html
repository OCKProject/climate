Model

Simulations for this study were conducted in the National Center for Atmospheric Research Community Atmosphere Model 5 (NCAR CAM5), the atmospheric component of the Community Earth System Model 1 52, coupled to a mixed-layer ocean. Mixed-layer coupling provides benefits in decreased computational intensity compared to full-ocean coupling, and has been found to lead to similar responses to full ocean coupling in the CESM model suite using an earlier version of the CESM atmospheric model 53. This similarity is expected to hold with use of CAM5 54. Simulations using CAM5 coupled to a slab ocean have been widely used in the peer-reviewed literature 54– 56, including to assess the climate response to aerosols 57, 58. NCAR CAM5 contains a fully interactive aerosol scheme, which transports and removes the emitted aerosols according to the model’s meteorology. We use the CAM5 model with its three mode modal aerosol module (MAM3) 59, containing internal mixing of black carbon, sulfate, and organic carbon with other aerosol species using the volume mixing rule. Refractive indices for sulfate and organic carbon are taken from Hess et al. 60 and for black carbon is taken from Bond et al. 61. CAM5 also includes aerosol indirect effects on clouds and the radiative effects of black carbon deposition on ice.
